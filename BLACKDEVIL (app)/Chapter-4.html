"Tuples"

Definition :
    *A tuple is a sequence of values, which can be of any type and they are indexed by integer.
    *It is immutable sequence of element. It cannot be altered.
    *Tuple and list are essentially the same. The important difference is that tuples are immutable.
    *A tuple is a comma-separated list of values.
    >>>t='a','b','c','d','e'
     *but it is not necessary to enclose tuples in parentheses.
    >>>t=('a','b','c','d','e')

"Dictionaries"


===>*It is an unordered collection of elements. An element in Dictionary has a key value pair enclosed with curly braces.
===>*Elements in Dictionary are accessed via keys and not by their position.
===>*The values of a Dictionary can be any datatype.
